This can be likened to how a referee in a sports game might blow their whistle to indicate that a foul has been committed. The sound of the whistle is the biological signal, and the act of blowing it is the conversion factor. In much the same way, when an external stimulus such as light or touch hits a sensory receptor cell, it results in the production of a biochemical messenger called a neurotransmitter.
